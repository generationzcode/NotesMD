#thermo 
* This seems like an odd topic to make a note on, but it's really important to understand
* Temperature is that quantity that is the same in two substances (regardless of what they are) when they are in thermal equilibrium. Thermal equilibrium is when there is not heat flow between them.
* This definition of temperature leads to all sorts of temperature scales but two things really narrow down the choices:
* One  - the scale should cause temperature to increase with the degree of "hotness"
* Two - the ratio of energy flow out to entropy change  is equal to temperature. This concept will be better understood after reading [[entropy]]
* From here we can define heat - the energy flow from a system due to temperature difference is heat. This, I expect, will anger any sane reader - how can temperature and heat have such self referencing definitions (is that the right term?). To that I'll join you. The scientists have really done some weird stuff
* Work is the other component of energy flow, which is characterised by mechanical motion between system boundaries
* heat and work are really fundamentally different in the sense, that all of work can be converted into heat energy, but the reverse is not possible
* The above gets infinitely more confusing when we get to entropy, so enjoy
