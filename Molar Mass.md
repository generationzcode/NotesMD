Molar mass of a homopolymer is $$M = xM_0$$
Molar mass of a copolymer is $$M  =\sum {x_iM_0^i}$$
Where $M_0$ is the molar mass of the repeating unit, and x is the degree of polymerisation of that particular repeating unit
## [[Molar Mass Distribution]]
Most polymers have a range of molar masses in the macromolecules that make up the crystal or amorphous or liquid substance. This is represented by a molar mass distribution
## Molar Mass Averages
* Number average molar mass where X represents mole fraction$$M_n = \sum{X_iM_i}$$
* Weight fraction, where N is number of molecules, is $$w_i = \frac{N_iM_i}{\sum{N_iM_i}}$$
* Weight averaged molar mass$$M_w = \sum{w_iM_i}$$
* There are tons of other molar mass average measures that I don't know about
* polydispersity index = Mw/Mn = PDI
* The higher the PDI, the higher the weight average contribution and the more variance in the molecular weight distribution
