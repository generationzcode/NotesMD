* Froude number = ND^2 rho / mu this is the ratio of inertia force to the gravitational force
* inertial force to surface tension is the weber number
* power number - ratio of applied force to mass times acceleration -> the higher the power number is the less efficient the mixing is powerwise
* Power number reduces with reyonolds number
* Baffling assures good mixing by providing a mixing pattern that follows throughout the batch
* Excessive baffling reduces the mass flow and localizes the mixing and may result in poor performance
* tank baffles must be reduces for viscous newtonian fluids which dont need them for proper loading and for non newtonian fluids that are pseudoplastic. This is because they tend to settle around the baffles, where the shear rate is low
* multiple radial flow impellers can cause flow patterns to merge (too far causes stragnant zones)and multiple axial flow impellers merely reinforce each others flow patterns
## Impeller design criteria
* size - diameter relative to the vessel, in viscous fluids, bigger size is generally required to ensure the entire tank is blended. Small diameter impellers are used at high speed for paint dispersion, etc
* Speed - RPM. This is related to size as mentioned above. The size kinda affects the speed as well
* Geometry - number, width, angle, shape, pitch, etc.
