### Petrol/Crude oil definiton
- petroleum - derived from the petra rock and oleum - oils
- It can be defined as naturally occurring organic material composed mainly of hydrocarbons in gaseous and liquid state
- Crude oil is a complex mixture of paraffins, naphthenes and aromatics - brownish dark in colour and colloidal in nature
- Composition of crude oil:
	- hydrogen - 10-14%
	- carbon - 83-87%
	- sulphur - 0.05 - 6%
	- nitrogen - 0.1 - 0.2%
	- oxygen - 0.05 - 2%
- Alkyl Naphthenes - $C_nH_{2n+2-0,2,4}$
## Alkanes
- we already know this so just the new stuff here - range from C1 to C100
- non reactive, non polar
- uniquely described by molecular formula until C3, and after C4 - isomers
- compounds above C16 are solid at room temperature and similar in nature to paraffin wax
- Paraffins are low in heavy graded oils
- Oxidation of C8-C30 results in fatty acids for soap industies
- oxidation of C12-C14 results in secondary alcohols
- Mono-chloro paraffins (C10-C30) as cutting oil additives, plasticizers and fire retardants
- sulphonated n-paraffins (C16-C17) for biodegradable detergents
- Cold flow properties of diesel and lubes
