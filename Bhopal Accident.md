#safety * After Bhopal incident, the process safety subject had a wake up call
* Lots of loss of life and property and the accident should never have happened
* Methyl isocyanate gas leaked and caused havoc. It's a very toxic gas
* There is a reaction - monomethyl amine+phosgene -> MIC, MIC+ alpha napthil -> carbaryl. 
* Union carbide put up a plant in india in the 30s. After 1980, the company started making the MIC onsite
* There were three MIC tanks, T1,T2 and T3, which was a spare tank. Each tank had a cooling jacket, water curtain. There was a scrubber and there was a flare. each tank had a pressure gauge, temperature sensor and level indicator
* Scrubber is an absorber, is any MIC escapes, the scrubber will absorb it. If anything escapes the scrubber, it goes to the flare
* 2nd december 1984, the night time, it was winters. When a pollutant enters atomosphere, the winds determine how far they'll go. If the winds flow at high speed, it will go very far away. An operator noticed a leak, he thought it was fine. Supervisor, an hour later, told the operator to check the piping and the valve. The valve had a leak. Another half an hour later, more smell came. The operator found the temp and pressure in the tank to be higher than usual. He thought it was due to gas being discharged by the people on the previous shift.
* A lot of time passed, they finally realized the weight of the problem. By then water came in contact with MIC and a vigorous runaway reaction started. On this day, no refrigeration unit working, water curtain malfunctioned, people forgot about T3, scrubber was out of service and flare was under repair
* The MIC escaped, the plant was near the railway station in the city and the gas was spread all over the city and people got irritated and there was no escape for them.
* Shift supervisor asked operator to wash the leak to find the leak. This is how water could have come into the stream.
## Errors
* Adequate in built safety systems were not provided and those provided were not well maintained
* Safe operating procedures (SOP) were not laid down and followed under strict supervision
* Total lack of onsite and offsite emergency control measures
* no hazard and operability study was carried out on the design
* evacuation drills were not held and practiced
* The community living near the plant were never warned about the dangers
* The local authorities weren't informed of the hazards and this couldn't respond effectively
* Signals were ignored
## Lessons 
* Dr Trevor Kletz rightly says, "What you don't have, you cant leak"
* Wherever and whenever possible reduce the inventories of hazardous materials
* Instead of making dangerous intermediaries, use a different paths
* People who are not there, cannot be killed, build factories outside of city limits
* HAZOP should be carried out
* Keep protective instruments in proper order and proper size
* Safe operating procedures must be maintained on a regular schedule 
* Training in loss prevention - loss prevention is an integral part of design, hazards should be removed in design instead of adding protective equipment
* Handling in offsite and onsite emergencies - a well planned and monitored "on site" emergency control plan is the responsibility of the individual industry and off site should be planned by the local authorities.
