## A note
- It is nearly impossible to have a "feel" for thermodynamics right away like with newtonian physics. 
- Thermodynamics is a subject that deals with static states. It is used to predict the states of systems given some constraints.
- This is very much like crystal ball gazing or hand reading in the sense that most of it is just metrics that the seer uses to get a final answer. (These metrics aren't arbitrary though...)
- To have a better understanding of this, the concept of "brilligence" in "An Introduction to General Systems Thinking" by GM Weinberg is a great book.
- Make no attempt to understand the metrics themselves(interpretations), only what their physical definitions are how they're related to each other.
