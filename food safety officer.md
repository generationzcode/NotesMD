> lower than designated officer

- bachelors in any of a bunch of subjects that have food somewhere in them
### What they can do
- seize the adulterant or other chemical if the business operator is unavailable. The adulterant has to be unsafe and they must know this from a sample they did analysis of. They also get to seal the premises for investigation
- Can stop a person from handling food if they are suffering from an infectious disease
### Their job
- To inspect all the people selling food in his area as frequently as the [[designated officer]] tells him to (or her)
- Check by himself that all the licenses are being followed by food businesses in his area
- Procure for analysis any food he thinks is bad according to information he receives from a purchaser or he just believes on the basis of manufacturing, stocking or selling methods
- Draw samples for the purposes of surveillance and all (these can't be used for prosecution)
- investigate complaints
- maintain a database of food businesses in his area
- a bunch of other stuff
