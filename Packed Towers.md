## Liquid Gas Contact
- Liquid, after inlet at the top of the tower should form a thin film over the packings, ideally
- In reality, the thickness of this film differs in the packings
- The liquid starts flowing in "rivulets" in the packing, leaving some area dry and some wet. This is called **Channeling**
## Pressure Drop
- The column experiences a pressure drop due to fluid friction. The gas pressure needed.
- At zero liquid inlet, the logarithmic graph of pressure drop per unit length vs superficial velocity is a straight line with a slope of 1.8
- As liquid flow rate increases, the line becomes curvier. This is because of liquid holdup.
- Until the graph curves, its straight with a higher pressure drop still due to fewer pathways for the gas as a lot of the volume is taken up by the liquid
- Liquid holdup is when the gas stops liquid from flowing downward. If the gas velocity is high enough, the column starts to flood with liquid as no liquid is let through. This is the flooding velocity
- The point at which liquid holdup starts to increase is called the loading point
