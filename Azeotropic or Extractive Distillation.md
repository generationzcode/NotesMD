> Extractive, Azeotropic and Reactive distillation are used to separate mixtures with close boiling points or relative unities close to one 

## Extractive
- ![[Pasted image 20250205144218.png]]
- Neat solution to the problem of similar relative volatilities
- You use a non volatile solvent that associates with one component of the mixture over the other and that causes the volatility of that component to decrease and the other is extracted
## Azeotropic Distillation
- same as extractive,except the entraining component is more volatile so it comes out in overhead - the entrainer forms a lower boiling azeotrope with one or more of the components in the feed
