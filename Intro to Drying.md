- It's the process of removing small amounts of liquid from a solid usually towards the end of of a series of operations
- It's cheaper to remove liquids mechanically as opposed to thermally
- When the product has absolutely no liquid content, it's called bone dry

## Classification
- Dryers are classified into adiabatic and non adiabatic dryers
>[!Info] Adiabatic
>These are also called direct dryers and use hot gas to dry products. They're pretty common in labs

>[!Info] Non Adiabatic
>Also called indirect dryers. They use steam or some other source to heat a medium like a metal surface on which the product is dried

- There also exist direct indirect dryers, which combing adiabatic and non adiabatic drying

## Handling of **Particulate** Solids
### Adiabatic
- Gas is blown across the surface of the solids, maybe on both sides (cross circulation)
- ![[Pasted image 20250325170915.png]]
- Gas is blown through a bed of coarse granular solids that are supported on a screen. (through circulation). Gas velocity kept low to keep particles from bleeding
- solids showered from above through gas
- gas fluidizes solid bed
### Non Adiabatic
- solids are spread over a heated horizontal surface and dried
- solids moved over inner surface of a cylinder or other shape using an agitator, screw, etc
