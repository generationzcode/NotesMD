- So we can't really use the expressions for reynolds stresses that we originally derived for [[Time Smoothed Equation of Change]]
- These arise from velocity fluctuations only, so no question of viscosity here
## Boussinesq's eddy viscosity
- Pretty simple, unusable in most cases. Eddy viscosity different at different points
- $$\tau^t_{yx} = -\mu^t\frac{d\overline v_x}{dy}$$
- $\mu^t$ is the eddy viscosity
## Prandtl's mixing length
- Apparently derived on bad assumptions
- $$\overline \tau_{yx}^t = -\rho l^2|\frac{dv_x}{dy}|\frac{dv_x}{dy}$$
- $l$ is the mixing length and it is proportional to distance from wall $y$
- $$l = \kappa_1y$$
- In the example, they just write it like:
- $$\tau_{yx} = -\rho\kappa_1^2y^2[\frac{dv_x}{dy}]^2$$
