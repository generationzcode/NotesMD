- The population is generated randomly with random "chromosomes"
- A chromosome is just a collection of characteristics that any individual possesses
- Each characteristic is called a gene
## Fitness function
- How close something is to what you want
- should be clearly defined, should pass sanity check, should produce a quantitative measure of just how fit any one individual is in any generation
## Mating and Mutating
- After evaluating the old generation, we make them mate (split of chromosomes to the child), the mating takes place based on fitness
- Two types of crossover methods in mating : single and multiple point
- In single point mating, a point is selected randomly and two children are generated by swapping the genes after that point, creating two children with genes opposite(? not really, but you get it...) to each other
- Multiple point is the above but more than one random point
- Since we want to add some new stuff to it all, we also randomly mutate bits in the chromosome
