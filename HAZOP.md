#safety 

>[!Quote]
>Its a systematic examination of a planned or existing process or operation in order to identify and evaluate problems that may present risks to personnel or equipment or prevent efficient operation

![[Pasted image 20240923111247.png]]
* Used mostly in chemical processes but now elsewhere as well
* This is a qualitative technique based on "guide words"
* Hazop was never done in [[Bhopal Accident]]
* Hazop studies may be done more extensively in the design stage, when the drawing are available, when the final piping and instrumentation designs are available, etc.
## Hazop team
* Team leader ->  defines the scope of the analysis, select the team members, plan and prepare the study, chair the HAZOP meetings
* Secretary -> make notes and all
* Project engineer, commissioning manager, process engineer, instrumentation engineer, safety engineer, etc.
## How do they do it?
* Divide the system into different sections
* Choose a study node - could be anything in specific like a pump, pipe, etc.
* Describe the design intent
* etc
## Safeguards
Facilities that reduce the frequency of the deviation from the set point of a certain phenomena or to mitigate it's consequences.
* Using a control system
* use sensors to detect the deviation and humans to rectify it
* prevent the deviation from occurring
* etc
## Process parameters
* flow, pressure, temperature, mixing, stirring, transfer, composition, addition, etc
## Guide words
* You use these to describe whether the process parameters are ok or not and in which direction
* More -> more than designed. eg. More Temperature
* No -> none of a parameter that should be some (?). Eg. No Flow
* etc.
