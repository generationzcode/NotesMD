 Take a sample from a population. The mean of that sample is a point estimate of the mean of the population and the function to get the mean is called the point estimator

A sample distribution is a distribution of the means of samples. mean of the sampling distribution is the same as population mean and variance is population variance/ sample size. The larger the sample, the smaller the variance. These parameters are then inserted into a normal distribution as the Z variable and we get the sample distribution

A point estimator is an unbiased estimator if it's expected value is equal to the population statistic

the point estimator with the smallest variance in it's sample distribution is called the minimum variance unbiased estimator

the sample mean is the MVUE for population mean.

## Obtaining estimator functions
How to get these estimators?
Use method of moments - you get moments of different orders that you can evaluate numerically
Use Maximum Likelihood Estimators (MLEs) by using a probability distribution and maximizing it with respect to the estimator you desire

Bayesian Estimators - I didn't fully understand these, but essentially you make a prior estimate of the mean and variance, use that along with variance to get the mean estimate using bayesian statistics.
