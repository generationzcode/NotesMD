> Production theory (part of it)

- Inputs and outputs are called factors of production. Land, labour, capital and raw materials
- Capital goods are goods that are themselves produced goods. I've read somewhere they're also defined as what is required to do production, so the goods required for production like machines and such. You know it when you see it
- Now because of the laws of physics and whatnot, we have constraints on what types of inputs can make an output. The set of realistic inputs to outputs is called the production set
- The production function gives the maximum possible output from the production set for the given input
- ![[Pasted image 20260117090245.png]]
- [[Cobb-Douglas]] also has a production function, check it out in the link
- An isoquant is the set of all inputs that can produce the same output
## The Marginal Product
- Just like it sounds, the amount of production increase for an increase in a factor of production. Just the derivative of the production function with respect to a factor
- The technical rate of substitution is how much another factor has to increase for a decrease in a factor to give the same amount of output
- $$\Delta x_2/\Delta x_1 = TRS$$
- $$\frac{MP_1(x_1,x_2)}{MP_2(x_1,x_2)}$$
## Diminishing rate of technical substitution
- This states that as you travel down the isoquant by increasing the amount of one good and decreasing another, the technical rate of substitution decreases
- So the slope of the isoquant must decreases as we increase $x_1$
- So the isoquants must have some sort of convex shape. 
- A convex function, we should have written somewhere is where the midpoint of the line between two points is higher than the function at that point
- A concave function is shown below, so a convex is the opposite of that
