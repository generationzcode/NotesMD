## Homopolymer
* Completely made of propylene monomer unit
* A semicrystalline polymer with less impact strength
* Used in tiffins, caps, etc
* shows tacticity - isotactic, syndiotactic and atactic
## Copolymer
* Why do we do copolymerization of polypropylene? It has a low impact strength
* Impact strength is how much of a collision or impact something can take
* Used in automotives, appliances
* In the stress strain curve, you have the elastic limit, yield point, the necking area - where the stress decreases with strain
* Mostly thermosets break before the yield point

# properties
- good impact strength at all operating temps
- shape stability at elevated temps
- Relatively inert to UV
- Low weight and costs
# Polymerization
## Bulk process
- occurs in liquid polyprop
- stirred tank reactor or loop reactor used
- zeigler natta catalyst used (usually TiCl4)
- 60-80 C and 30-40 atm
- easy polymer recovery
