## Clarifying filters
* Clarifying filters - small amount of solids are filtered out to produce clean gas or sparkingly clear liquids. 
* The solids are trapped inside the filter medium and on it's external surface
## Crossflow filters
* Crossflow filters - the feed suspension flows under pressure at high velocity
* A thin layer of solids may form on the surface of the medium but it's not permanent because the liquid velocity keeps regenerating the surface
* the pores of the filter medium are small enough to exclude most of the suspended particles
* Some liquid passes through filter medium (clear filtrate). More concentrated suspension remain behind
