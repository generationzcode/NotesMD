## Process safety culture
* a combination of group values and behaviors that determine the manner in which process safety is managed
* maintain a sense of vulnerabilitey in your organization to process events
* empower individuals to fulfill their safety responsibilities. Give them the necessary authority and resources 
* Defer to expertise
* open comms
* questioning/learning environment
* mutual trust in managers and employees

# Pillar 2 - Understand risks
## Process knowledge management
* Process knowledge refers to the written documents, drawings, specifications, selection of safe operating limits, MSDS
* this should be well catalogued and understood by many
## Hazard identification and risk analysis
* this is also called process hazard analysis (pha)
* A PHA consists of a HAZOP to evaluate hazards and consequences, LOPA to evaluate likelihood
* FMECA - failure, modes, effects and criticality analysis also is a semi quantitative method to measure likelihood
# Pillar 3 - Manage Risk
## Operating Procedures
* there are written instructions that list the steps for a given task , and describe the manner in which the steps are to be performed. 
* These are needed
## Safe work practices
* stuff that isn't part of operations but you need to do every now and then has to be done safely
* If you're welding on a tank which may have flammable residue, dont light it up to make sure it won't explode when you weld (the logic doesnt work no matter how you turn it about)
## Asset integrity
* Strong pillars and supports and well designed and reliable equipment
* Safety equipment must be checked regularly to make sure they don't fail when they're needed
* sensors must be calibrated every once in a while. When an alarm blares, assume the sensor is working perfectly
## Contractor Management
* Select the proper contractors, give them enough safety information
* check their records, establish roles for your safety program with the contractors, ensure their training and supply appropriate information to them
## Training and Performance Assurance
* Practical instruction in job is needed
* Performance assurance is methods in which personnel demonstrate their job competency 
## Management of Change
* Ensure changes don't produce new hazards
* A change is anything that changes the process safety information
## Operational readiness
* Do startup and shutdown checklists properly
## Conduct of Operations
* the day to day operations should be well planned out
## Emergency management
* Planning for possible emergencies
* Providing resources to execute the plan
# Pillar 4 : learning from experience
* Incident investigations should be done after they occur
* measurement and metrics - use these to monitor how well your RBPS management system is working
* auditing - get someone from outside to look at your plant
* Management Review and Continuous Improvement - evaluate whether management systems are performing as intended
