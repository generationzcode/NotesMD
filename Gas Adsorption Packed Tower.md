## Pressure Drop
> This was studied in the experiment on hydrodynamics of packed towers

- As far as  know, the top of the tower is at atmospheric pressure, so the pressure drop is basically the pressure buildup at the bottom
- ![[Pasted image 20250324095426.png]]
- The log log graph of the air mass velocity vs del P/H
- When it's dry, the line is straight, the pressure increases with 1.8th power of the velocity
- The non dry sections have an increasing slope with increase in air mass velocity for the pressure drop since as the velocity increases, they start "holding up" liquid
- The point at which the slope changes with mass velocity is called the loading point
- if the gas flow is increased enough, liquid at parts becomes the continuous phase in the tower, causing flooding
