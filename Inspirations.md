## Philosophy and Logic
- Abduction - getting a conclusion from an observation and some domain knowledge
- Induction - finding rules from observations
- Deduction - finding observations from rules
## Psychology (bunch of -isms)
- Structuralism - simplifies the mind into the simplest definable components then joines them to make something complex
- Functionalism - focuses on the utility of behavior over years of human existence (on evolution)
- Behaviorism - Behaviors are either reflexes due to stimuli or consequence of individual's history of punishment and reward
- Gestalt Theory - Mind forms a global whole with self organizing tendencies (??)
- Reductionism - Mind can be reduced to a bunch of low level operations when combined together make sense (ANNs)
- Cognitivism - Mind can be thought of as a symbolic AI carrying out logical tasks
## Categorization
- Classical view - they're arbitrary, with defining attributes. The intension (set of defining attributes) determines the extension of a category (members)
- Modern view - motivated by their properties in the system around us (not arb), built around central members sharing certain attributes with the members over nonmembers. 
## Neuroscience
- Principle of locality - tasks specific to local areas of brain as opposed to holism
- karl lashley - specific behaviours can be taken over by any part of the brain (plasticity), law of mass action (brain damage proportional to tissue damaged), equipotentiality - all parts of the brain have potential to be all parts of the brain
- Hebb's work - neurons that fire together wire together blah blah blah
- Hubel and Wiesel - visual cortexes have neurons that respond to small regions of the visual field
