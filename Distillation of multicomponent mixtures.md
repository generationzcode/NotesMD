## Intro to multicomponent
* One component is called he light key component and the other is called the heavy key component
* What doesn't appear in the bottom or appears the least in the bottom is called the light key component 

| Feed | Top Product | Bottoms |
| ---- | ----------- | ------- |
| A    | A           |         |
| B    | B           | B       |
| C    | C           | C       |
| D    |             | D       |
* B is the lightest component, not appearing in the bottoms and is thus called the light key component
* C is the heaviest component appearing in the distillate and is thus called the heavy key component
## Fenske Method
> For reasoning go to [[Fenske's Method]], it's given for binary mixtures, but it could be applied here too...
* Used to compute the minimum number of stages under total reflux conditions if the relative volatility remains constant
* $$n+1 = \frac{log(\frac{x_A}{x_B}_D\frac{x_B}{x_A}_W)}{log(\alpha_{AB})_{av}}$$
* It is assumed that the reboiler acts as a theoretical stage (partial reboiler)
* A is the lightkey component and B is the heavy key component
## Underwood Method
* Used to compute the minimum reflux ratio required for a desired separation if the relative volatility remains reasonably constant
* $$\frac{\alpha_Ax_{fA}}{\alpha_A-\theta}+\frac{\alpha_Bx_{fB}}{\alpha_B-\theta}+\frac{\alpha_Cx_{fC}}{\alpha_C-\theta}+... = 1-q$$
* $$\frac{\alpha_Ax_{dA}}{\alpha_A-\theta}+\frac{\alpha_Bx_{dB}}{\alpha_B-\theta}+\frac{\alpha_Cx_{dC}}{\alpha_C-\theta}+... = R_{min}+1$$
* q is the ratio of heat required to vaporise one mole of the feed to the molar latent heat of the feed
* $\alpha_{A,B,C}$ are the relative volatilities of the components relative to the least volatile component
* $\theta$ is the root of the equation, which lies between $\alpha_A$ and $\alpha_B$ - ==need to check this out==
### Binary mixtures
* $$R_{min} = \frac{1}{\alpha-1}[\frac{x_d}{x_f}-\alpha\frac{1-x_d}{1-x_f}]$$
* $\alpha$ is the relative volatility with respect to the least volatile component
## Gilliland Method
* Gives the empirical relation between the reflux ratio,R, and the number of plates,n
* Only the minimum reflux ratio $R_min$ and the number of plates at total reflux, $n_{min}$ are required
