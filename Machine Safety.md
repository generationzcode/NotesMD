#safety Unguarded moving parts of machines and the sudden or uncontrolled release of their power systems can result in serious injuries

## Causes of accidents while working with machinery
* Loose clothing, hair, jewelry
* Materials ejected from the machine when it is operational
* Inadvertent starting of the machine
* slipping and falling into a place
* Contact with sharp edges
* Making adjustments while the machine is operational
* Unauthorized operation of machine
* Lack of preventative maintenance 
## Hazards
* Rotating gears
* Belt and pulley
* Chain and sprocket 
* Between the grinding wheel and tool rest
* Between rotating and fixed parts
Machines alone:
* Shafts, couplings
A nip point is a dangerous part of a machinery

## Machine guarding
* Any machine part which can cause injury must be guarded
* Fixed guard - doesnt move, kept in place permanently by fasteners
* Interlocked Guard - Shuts off or disengages power to the machine and prevents it from starting when the guard is removed or opened
* Adjustable Guard - Provides a barrier which can be adjusted to suit the varying sizes of the input stock
* Self adjusting guard - Provides a barrier which moves according to the size of the stock entering the danger site (?)
* Pull back device - The device is attached to the wrist of the operator which pulls the operators hand away from the point of operation
* Two hand control - used so that people are forced to use both hands so they don't multitask
## Other safeguarding aids
* Shields can be used to provide protection from flying particles
* Holding tools can be used to place and remove stock instead of having to use your hand in a danger area
## Precautions
* Ensure that the guards are in position and in good working order when working
* Didn't catch the rest
* Keys and adjusting wrenches must be removed from the machine before operating it
* Stop the machine before measuring, cleaning or making any adjustments
* Do not handle metal turnings by hand as they can cause injury. Use brush or rake to remove turning
* Keep hands away from the cutting head and all moving parts
* Cutting tools must be clean and sharp
* Avoid awkward operations and hand positions
* Keep work area clean. Floor must be level and have a non slip surface
* There must be enough space around the machine to do the job safely
* The person working with the machine must not be distracted
* Machines must not be unattended. Switch it off
* Rotating parts must not be stopped with hands
* Compressed air must not be used to clean machines as this can force small particles to fly off and can cause injury to the eyes
## Personal protective equipment
* Safety glasses must be used when working with dangerous stuff
* Safety glasses must be work by all personnel entering an area thats dangerous
* Ear protection must be work for protection from high noise
* Hand gloves must NOT be used while working with machines, due to the changes of getting caught at nip point

* When making deep holes, make sure the hole is clean properly
* Lubricate the drill bit before drilling a hole
* Reduce the drilling pressure when the drill begins to break through the material
* DO NOT force the drill with extra pressure

* Centre the drill work deeply enough to provide support for the piece while it's turning
* Secure and clamp the piece while it's being worked
* Guard must be provided to the chuck
* Inspect chuck for wear or damage
* Remove the chuck wrench immediately after adjusting the chuck

* Holding the work incorrectly (grinding machine problems)
* Grinding on the side of the wheel is badd
* Using the wrong type of wheel or disk


## Lockout, tagout
Lockout - tagout or lock and tag is a system used to ensure that machines are properly shut off and not started up again before the completion  of maintenance or servicing work.

Hazardous power sources must be isolated before repair can be started
