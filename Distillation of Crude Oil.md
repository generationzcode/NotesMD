### Gas
- The gas at the top is used in the furnace to power the distillation unit
- 7-10% of the crude that is refined goes to power the refinery itself
- It's also known as unstabilised naptha
## Sour gas
- When you put in steam from the bottom of the column, you don't want it to condense anywhere
- If it condenses, you start getting HCl or other acids in the distillation column - which causes corrosion
- So when the water condenses in the reflux drum, it's "sour" or acidic since it has H2S and all

## Pinch Technology
-  A method for systematically analysing processes and the surrounding utility systems with the first and second laws of thermodynamics
- The first laws states that energy in = energy out
- Second law - heat flows through positive heat difference
- For any heat exchanger network, both these laws must be satisfied
### Heat Intergeration
- objective is to minimise the number of shells or surface area for the heat duty
- Incremental surface is cheaper than more shell
- Avoid temperature crosses (the heating fluid cannot be cooler than the heated fluid)
- do not match streams with large differences in heat content or volume
- Whenever you design something, you need to know how to implement. If it cannot be started up easily, do not integrate
### Application of pinch technology
- It determines the minimum amount of energy required to run the process
- it can determine the beneficial changes to the process
