## Sources of Energy
* Spectroscopy needs energy, absorption and scattering spectroscopy get this from photons. Emission and luminescence use thermal, radiant (photon) or chemical energy
* Continuum source - Emits radiation over a wide range of wavelengths
* Line source - Emits radiation only at select wavelengths
* Flame and plasma sources are used for thermal energy
* Chemical energy is provided by exothermic or chemiluminescent reactions
* In a continuum source, nominal wavelength is the wavelength which the wavelength selector is set to pass (its not exactly that, since its continuum)
* Effective bandwidth - the width of the band of radiation passing through a wavelength selector measured at half the bands height
* Resolution - separation between two spectral features, narrow effective bandwidth increases resolution
* FIlters narrow the bandwidth

* Interferometer has a beam splitter which causes the source to recombine with radiation from a moving mirror, causing many wavelengths to engage in constructive/destructive interference, the outcome of which is converted using a fourier transform into a normal spectrum
