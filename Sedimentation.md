# Stuff I noted down during class
- clarifier is when you want one component very pure
- classifier is when you want to simply separate two phases
- if the equipment is used for thickening purpose, it's called a thickener
- flocculation is when solids suspended in a liquid agglomerate and float up
- coagulation is flocculation but the stuff settles down instead
- precipitation - insoluble solids formed from reaction
## Terminal velocity
- free settling: motion of particles is not affected by other particles or walls of the vessel
- hindered settling: Settling is affected by interference of other particles
### Hindered settling
- settling velocity is a function of turbulent settling velocity and voidage
- $$u_h=u_t\epsilon^n$$
- u_h is the hindered settling velocity
- u_t is the terminal settling velocity
- \epsilon is voidage
- n is an experimental quantity, related to reynolds number, particle size and vessel diameter

## Kynch Theory
 - **Assumptions**:
 - Particle concentration is uniform
 - There are no wall effects
 - There is no differential settling of particles as a result of differences in shape, size or composition
 - Settling velocity of particles depends only on the local concentration of the particles
 - Initial concentration is uniform and increases towards the bottom
 - Settling velocity tends to 0 as concentration approaches a limiting values corresponding to that of deposited layer at the bottom of vessel
