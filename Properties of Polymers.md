* Polymers are always covalently bonded
* With high inductive effect, low molar mass molecules are liberated as the electronegative group abstracts hydrogen from the carbon
* Primary bonding - covalent and ionic
* Secondary - Configuration and conformation
* Configuration - you cant change this without breaking the bonds
* Conformation - this is related to the rotation of the bonds
* Tacticity - relating to the stereochemistry of repeating units
* Tacticity - isotacticity, atacticity and syndiostereotacticity

* Glass transition temperature - when you heat the polymer, there is a transition phase, called glass transition. Mostly polymers are semicrystalline or amorphous - glass transition is when the polymer is in amorphous state. The lower this temperature, the more flexible the polymer is. Used in thermoplastic. Above $T_g$, polymer is flexible and below it it is glassy. $T_g$ is the temperature below which the polymer is mostly rigid
* Tg causes a second order phase change
* ![[Pasted image 20241004101559.png]]
* The $T_g$ is the temperature when the specific volume vs temperature graph has a change in slope
* DSC - differential scanning calorimetry, can find the $T_c$, $T_m$, $T_g$, gel point, everything
* DMTA - dynamic mechanical and thermal analysis
* DSC and DMTA are two techniques to get the $T_g$ value
* Melting temperature - Used in thermoplastic. $T_m$
* Melting temperature - related to heat gain
* Crystallization temperature, $T_c$ - Used in thermoplastic. When you cool the polymer it starts crystallizing. When the entropy change is the highest - peak crystallization temperature
* Semi crystalline polymer shows $T_m$, $T_c$ and $T_g$. Amorphous only shows $T_g$
* Semi Crystalline polymers is defined by how regular the packing of the polymer is. 
* Amorphous Polymers have a completely irregular packing
* Gel point - Used in thermoset. When the degree of polymerization of a thermoset tends to infinity
* Curing temperature - Used in thermoset. When the polymer cures

## Structural Properties
* In case of polymers, there are two types of crystals - lamella, and spherulites
* Lamella - forms around the nucleation point, the chain folds
* The many lamella form spherulite, spherulites are big spherical crystals
* If the rate of cooling is high, crystals arent formed well. Slow rate of cooling forms good polymers
