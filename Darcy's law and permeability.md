* Darcy showed that average velocity was directly proportional to $\Delta P$ and inversely proportional to bed thickness
* This is expressed as Darcy's law:
* $$V_{av} = K\frac{\Delta P}{L_{tube}}$$
* The linear relation between flow rate and pressure indicates that the flow is laminar
* This is because the reynolds number through the pore spaces is low
* Resistance to flow arises mainly from viscous drag:
* $$V_{av} = B\frac{\Delta P}{\mu L_{tube}}$$
* B is the permeability coefficient. It's completely dependent on the properties of the packed bed
 
> [!Info] 
> In real life, flow isn't always laminar, it can also be turbulent with a high enough head. Many scientists have developed turbulent derived equations 

> Derivations are a part of the syllabus and will be asked in the examination
