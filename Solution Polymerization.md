#polymers 
Monomer and initiator are placed in a solvent, where they react
Solves the heat transfer and viscosity issue with [[Bulk Polymerization]] but causes new issues like chain transfer to solvent. 
To isolate the polymer from the solvent, we either evaporate it off and if the polymer is insoluble in the solvent, we precipitate it off. Both these processes are inefficient and aren't used widely in industry

Prof's PPT
* Heat removed during polymerization can be facilitated by conducting the polymerization in an organic solvent or water
* Both the initiator and the monomer must be soluble in it
* the solvent must have acceptable chain transfer characteristics and suitable melting and boiling points for the conditions of the polymerization and subsequent solvent removal step
* Solvent choice may be influences by other factors such as flash point, cost and toxicity
* Reactors are usually stainless steel or glass lined
