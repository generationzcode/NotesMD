* When fluidisation just starts, its called minimum or incipient fluidisation
* The del P per unit length decreases on start of fluidisation since the length increases since the particles move upwards
* In fluidization you do not want to carry away the solids.
* When a fluid flows slowly upwards through a bed of very fine particles, the flow is streamline and a linear relation exists between the pressure gradient and flow rate
* If log(delP/Ltube) is plotted against log(Vavg), a straight line of slope 1 is obtained
* In a fluidised bed, total frictional force on particles = effective weight of bed
* Thus in a bed of cross sectional area A, depth Ltube, and porosity epsilon, additional pressure drop across bed due to layout weight of particles is given by:
* $$ \Delta p = (1-\epsilon)(\rho_P-\rho)L_{tube}g $$
* The area cancels out
* This relationship applies between the initial expansion of the bed till the transport of the bed
