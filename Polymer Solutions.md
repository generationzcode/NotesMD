- A solution is anything that has a solute and a solvent
- The solute is a polymer here
- The solvent is used to make a dilute solution. 
- Dilution is done to find viscosity and such
- Solutions are made for [[Processing]] techniques like spinning fibers. The fiber can be spun from melt, but it can also be spun from a solution
## Dissolution
- Low molecular weight polymers are easy to dissolve
- The higher ones you get in granules have molecular weights in the hundreds of thousands, they're tougher to dissolve
- When the interaction between the polymer molecules within the polymer (intramolecular), the solvent fits into empty spaces within the polymer and it "swells" up. 
- Some polymers just take a lot of time, some need higher temperature to dissolve at normal timescales (most polyolefins)
- Polyesters, Polyamides dissolve quickly since they're polar.

## Solubility Properties
- So they start from a small concentration, slowly add polymer and see the solubility limit and time taken to dissolve
- Mostly what we want to know is the viscosity of the solution.
- Poissons equation: $$\eta  = \frac{\pi \Delta P R^4}{8Q L}$$
- $\mu = \eta$ both are regular viscosity 
- Types of viscosity: specific, relative, reduced and inherent.
- $$\eta_{reduced} = \frac{\eta_{sp}}{C}$$
### Viscosity
- We'll have to study this better, but there are a couple of types of viscosity
- Specific, relative, reduced, inherent and intrinsic.
- $$\eta_{rel} = \eta/\eta_0$$ where $\eta$ is viscosity of solution and $\eta_0$ is the viscosity of the solvent
- Other forms I don't have here
### Kreamer's eqn
- $$\ln{n_{rel}/C} = \eta-k_k[\eta]^2C$$
