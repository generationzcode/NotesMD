- Flows in pipes above the reynolds number of 2100 are generally observed to have turbulent regime. Laminar flow can be maintained above this number if the pipe is smoother than ==Pitbull's bald head==
- Turbulent flow is chaotic. This means the velocities aren't uniform like in laminar flow, but having a lot of random fluctuations, $v = \bar v+v'$. 
- ![[Pasted image 20251027204132.png]]
- Because we really don't know how to make equations of change behave this way, we need to *smooth* out the velocities. So take an average of it over time, this way you end up with a [[Time Smoothed Equation of Change]]
- This EoC has a [[Turbulent Momentum Flux]] and unlike a laminar flow, it cannot be expressed with [[Newtons Law of Viscosity]], since it's not just related to the velocity gradient.
