## Biohazards
* hazards related with the pathogen or human/animal cells being used in research
* related with the procedures and practices followed in tha lab
* researchers who manipulate human/animal cells are at risk of possible exposure to potentially infectious pathogens that may be present in those cells/tissues
* human cell lunes may contain blood borne pathogens which can be transmitted due to improper handling
* Inhalation of aerosols
* Contact of the agent with the skin, eyes or mucus membrane
* Inocculation by contaminated drugs
* Bites from infected animals or contact with their body fluids
## aerosols
can be generated when:
pipetting
Blending
Centrifugation
Sonication
These respirable size particles when inhaled are retained in the lungs and cause infection

## Classification of pathogenic microorganisms
* Risk group 1 - won't cause any infection in humans/animals. Least risky
* Risk group 2 - can cause disease in humans/animals but won't be a serious hazard like ecoli and all
* Risk group 3 - A pathogen that can cause serious animal or human disease but wont spread. Foot and mouth disease virus
* Risk group 4 - pathogen that usually causes serious human or animal disease and can be readily transmitted from one individual to another
## Biological containment
any combination of vector and host must be chosen to limit the the infectivity of vector to specific hosts and control the host vector survival in the environment
