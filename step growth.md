The backlink (polymerization) describes how to differentiate between step and chain growth by analysis of reaction kinetics

Basically the chain length is a function of conversion of the step growth reaction, as opposed to chain growth where the chain length is independent of conversion

Step growth occurs by successive reactions of mutually reactive functional groups. The reactive mechanisms are usually simple. This is the easiest and most commonly understood kind of polymerization (my opinion)

Mathematical analysis of these reactions have been done first by Carothers (fairly simplistic, not very accurate) then a proper statistical analysis. No shade to Carothers though

Step growth reactions are divided into polycondensation and polyaddition. The condensation reaction the loss of a molecule such as water during the polymerization, this loss does not occur in addition reactions (dominated by unsaturated molecules as you would imagine)
