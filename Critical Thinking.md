## Socratic Questions
![[Pasted image 20241011145250.png]]
* These are "at the heart of critical thinking", to unde
* Questions for clarification: clarifying the thinking behind a certain concept - "Why do you say that?", "Could you explain further?", "How does the concentration suddenly reduce on entry of a stream to a CSTR", "Whats the blow off cock there for in a boiler?", "How does the triple product of these partial derivatives equal -1?", "Whats the physical significance of exergy, gibbs free energy, enthalpy,etc"
* Questions that probe assumptions - "Why is there no temperature gradient from the boundary layer to the center of the fluid in the pipe?", "Why does entropy always increase?"("just cus")
* Questions that probe reasons and evidence - "Could the cold vodka be cause for the antidote not neutralizing the poison?"
* Questions about viewpoints and perspectives - "Why don't we use exergy balance here instead of gibbs energy balance?", "Why did maxwell come up with his relations in thermodynamics? What was going on in his mind? Why did he feel the need for it?", "When should I use -this concept- over -that concept-"
* Questions about implications and consequences: "If I did exergy balance for this instead of energy balance, what would be different? Isn't it still correct?"
* Questions about the question: "What was the point of this question?", "Was that question easy?", "Would we use the concept of entropy in this question?", "How does this question relate to the lecture taken today?"
## Critical thinking actions
* Basically analyzing, applying standards, discriminating, information seeking, logical reasoning, predicting and transforming knowledge over different situations
* These actions aren't too helpful to a student since it's difficult to use this list to develop a critical understanding of a subject. This is probably for teachers to assess students and all
## Using the above stuff
* For the theory, ask socratic questions (good ones)
* For the questions - ask another question or different calculations for the problem, think of all the ways you could solve the problem incorrectly (and why they're incorrect), think of how to make the problem easier, more difficult or more exciting, make a list of concepts taught in the problem and what the point of the problem was, vary parameters of the questions and study their consequences, try to come up with better ways of solving the problem (more elegant ways - my own addition)
