#safety 
> This subject is actually pretty cool
* Safety - its the use of technologies to prevent disasters before they occur
* Hazard - a chemical or physical condition that has the potential to cause damage
* Risk - A measure of human injury, environmental damage or economic loss in terms of both incident likelihood and magnitude of loss or injury
* Engineering ethics - with the given resources, engineers must make a safe and secure working environment. This is a responsibility to society
## Statistics
* Important statistics in weighing the merits and demerits of different safety systems:
* OSHA incidence rate - number of worker injuries per 100 worker years. One worker year is assumed to contain 2000 worker hours. 
* OSHA incidence rate can also be on the basis of lost workdays
* Fatality rate (per year)
* Fatal Accident Rate - Used by the british chemical industry, number of fatalities per 1000 employees working a full lifetime, so FAR is 10^8 worker hours

* Accident stats can be misleading as major causes of death like the bhopal incident are in one single event
* Acceptable levels of risk, for any procedure - if the plant has more than one procedure (always...), the levels of risk must be much much less than the levels for normally sitting at home, since the risks of exposure form multiple processes can be additive
## Accidents
> Chemical Accidents follow typical patterns

* Mostly comprise of fires, explosions and vapour cloud explosions. Also a little bit of windstorms and flooding
* ![[Pasted image 20241104221929.png]]
* Explosions cause a lot of economic loss. Vapour cloud explosions occur when a large cloud of volatile and flammable vapour is released unconfined in the whole plant, then ignited
* Toxic release causes little capital loss, however, lots of lawsuits and deaths
*  Almost all accidents not caused by natural disaster can be attributed to human error or mechanical error. Other causes can be process upsets, design or sabotage
* piping systems, storage tanks followed by reactor piping systems are the biggest locations of accidents
* Accidents follow a three step process: initiation, propagation and termination

## Inherent safety
* An inherently safe plant relies on process chemistry and physics rather than  control systems, interlocks, redundancy and all to stop accidents
* layers of safety above the process are of no interest to inherent design (they are needed, but inherent design focuses mostly on the first layer, which is the process itself)
* Inherent design is described using four words - minimize(intensification), substitute, moderate(attenuation and limitation of effects) and simplify(simplification and error tolerances)
* **minimize** - reduce storage and usage of hazardous chemicals in process, if possible, they should be produced and consumed in situ
* **substitute** - use different materials in the process
* **moderate** - use the hazardous materials in less hazardous conditions, such as refrigerated, in low vapour pressure, using larger particle sizes to reduce dust issues (flash explosions)
* **simplify** - make the plant simpler by : designing controls to be placed in a friendly, logical manner, separating processes to prevent domino effect, etc

## Significant Events
### Flixborough, England
* Nypro limited produced 70,000 tonnes a year of **caprolactam**, used in production of nylon
* Process used cyclohexane, which volatilizes immediately in atmospheric conditions
* six reactors in series oxidising cyclohexane to cyclohexanol using inhected air in the presence of a catalyst. Each reactor containing 20 tonnes of cyclohexane
* reactor 5 had a leak - a vertical crack in it's stainless steel structure. They removed the reactor for repairs. They then connected reactor 4 to reactor 6
* Actual pipes were 28 inches in diameter, they only had 20 inches. They made a workaround and created a bypass. 
* Bypass didnt have enough support, overflexed, and ruptured
* 30 tons of cyclohexane were freed, forming a vapour cloud, which was then ignited, levelling the entire facility and killed a number of people

## ARCO channelview, peroxides
* Explosion occurred at the wastewater tank at the ARCO chemical plant in channelview, texas
* Oxygen analyser didnt detect flammable atmosphere in the tank, (due to buildup of peroxides), the flammable vapours got sucked into the compressor when it restarted and ignited
* this happened during maintenance 
* proper MOC procedures should have been followed before maintenance work
## Terra Industries, ammonium nitrate
* Ammonium nitrate explosion, port neal iowa
* Process shut down, AN was left in vessels
* The nitric acid plant was shut down, to prevent backflow of AN into the nitric acid line, 200 psig steam was applied through a nitric acid sparger.
* AN is more sensitive to decomposition at higher temperatures, other plants were stricter about steam sparges
* Process safety knowledge is the RBPS element causing this
* There should have been a HAZOP study, so another element could be "Process Knowledge Management" and "HAZOP"
## Partridge Raleigh, Welding Incident
* Tank of crude oil that had been emptied had to be connected via pipe to another tank
* Workers from outside came to weld, welding caused ignition of flammable vapours
* Welder was the only one out of 4 wearing a harness, so he was injured while the others died
* Contractor management is the RBPS element here
## Formosa Plastic, Vinyl chloride release
* Explosion and fire killed five workers
* PVC stored began burning
* Vinyl chloride monomer along with other components are heated under pressure to make PVC
* Worker confused which reactor was in operation and which wasn't because of plant layout
* Worker overrode the interlock system to drain the reactor as he thought it was faulty
* VCM poured out, building filled with vapour and liquid
* Shift foreman took measures to slow  release rather than evacuate
* Operating procedures needed
## Hoeganaes - Dust flash fire
* Plant converted scrap metal to powders
* Powders werent cleaned periodically
* Portions of dust ignited several times, causing flash fires in the air
* RBPS element - understanding and managing risks, the second pillar of RBPS
