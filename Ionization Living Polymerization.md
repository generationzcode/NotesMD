## Cationic living polymerizations
* Basically we want to stop chain transfer to monomer, the monomers used in cationic polymerization are often nucleophillic and the carbocation stands no chance against their attack
* To reduce this, we change the carbocation to a carbo-sometimes-cation. It is essentially end capped with a covalent bond which is weak enough to break sometimes to cause propagation then again stop it
* This is not true living polymerization, but it's good enough (it's reversible deactivation living polymerization)

## Anionic living polymerizations

These polymerz, they live it up
no terminationnn ZERO
* As we've seen in [[Anionic Ionization Polymerization]], there are no inherent termination mechanisms, however, dead chains **are** still generated by chain transfer to monomer. If we can remove this chain transfer, we get real, living, polymers. They never die
* There are two (in the book, irl probably many more) ways to generated living anionic polymers: [[Electron Transfer Polymerization]] and [[Group Transfer Polymerization]]
