Ok it's not actually that confusing - chain transfer happens when the active site of a polymer is given away to a different molecule, effectively making it a dead chain. It's like termination, except the active site still live on
![[Pasted image 20240913174804.png]]
You also have chain transfer to polymers other than solvents, monomers and initiators and that just makes it branch since the active site goes to some random part of the polymer chain, not the ends. 

Catalytic chain transfer - uses a cobalt complex to cause chain transfer from a radical with hydrogen from an alpha methyl group  transferred from the active polymer (making it dead) to a monomer, causing it to become an active site
