## Basics
* Density is a point function. We assume it's value to vary continually throughout the fluid. Such a fluid is called a continuum.
* A continuum is where the difference in properties between points is so smooth that we can use differential calculus to study it
* If we look at a volume smaller than the **Limiting Volume**, molecular variations ruin our results
* If we look at a volume larger than the limiting volume, aggregating variations ruin our results
* To find the density of a fluid:$$\rho = \lim_{\delta V \rightarrow \delta V^{*}}\frac{\delta m}{\delta V}$$ Where m is mass, V is volume and $V^*$ is limiting volume
