
* for solid suspension, the flow velocity must be higher than the solid settling velocitu otherwise suspension wouldn't occur
* Axial flow impellers are best suited for such purposes
* free settling solids settle at above 0.3m/s, hindered settle at lower rates
* free settling solids settle at such rates when there are no other fine particles to hinder their natural settling velocities
* Hindered settling is rare, most applications have free settling solids and it becomes necessary to produce vertical velocity components through impellers
* Degree of suspension - complete on bottom motion(all move same), complete off bottom suspension(all move in different directions), complete uniformity(suspended uniformly)
* the higher Z/T, the more power is needed for suspension
* Dish bottom tanks are good for such work, sloped bottoms cause solids to collect at the bottom
