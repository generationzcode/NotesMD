
# CRE
*  Macromixing is when aggregates form on mixing so the aggregates mix,not the molecules themselves
* Micromixing is when the molecules mix perfectly
* Real life is somewhere between these two extremes
* Macromixing occurs when trying to mix two viscous substances
* IRL, mostly everything is taken to be micro-mixed but the study of macro mixtures is important for heterogenous reactions
* Macro and micro fluids show same performance in batch and plug flow reactor
* Macro and micro fluids show different performance in CSTRs because of the exit time distribution and how it affects reaction rates for second order reactions
# Extra
process design, impeller power characteristics and mechanical design are the three elements of mixer design. 
* Process design is concerned with the fluid regime required, impeller fluid mechanics and the translation to various vessel sizes
* Impeller power characteristics - relate the combined effects of fluid properties, impeller geometry, impeller diameter and speed to power consumption
* Mechanical design is stuff like drive, shaft sealing device, etc
