* this is further divided into 4 types:
* Linear low density polyethylene (LLDPE)
* Low density polyethylene (LDPE)
* High density polyethylene (HDPE)
* High molecular weight polyethylene (HMWPE)
## LLDPE
![[Pasted image 20250123142321.png]]
	photo credits to dow chemicals
* Short chain branches, copolymer of octene and ethylene
* range of 115 to 130C melting point
* used in both rigid and flexible applications
## LDPE
* Long chain branches
* made using a high pressure process
* melting temp of LDPE is 105 to 115C
## HDPE
* made using a low pressure process
* 130-145C melting point
* 0.94-0.95 g/cm3
* most inert polymer chemically
* Used to store highly basic to highly acidic stuff
## HWMPE
* 0.9g/cm3
* when you are extracting the fibre, while slowly cooling it, you create one of the strongest fibres known to mankind
* **DSM chemicals manufactures this**
