Linear step polymerizations can be represented by:
* $$RA_2+RB_2$$
* $$ARB$$
* and a bunch of other combinations the the above terms. The R is basically the everything in the molecule but the functional group and the letters A and B represent the functional groups. There are clearly two functional groups on each representation above. If two of the same letter are on R , like $RA_2$, it means both the functional groups are the same, like in ethylene gylcol or terepthalic acid. ARB means the functional groups on the molecule are different and the molecule basically reacts with itself
*  A and B are mutually reactive
## Polycondensations
### [[Polyesters]]
* Happens when carboxylic acid or acid halide reacts with alcohol group
* Reaction between the carboxylic acid and the alcohol groups is slow and has to be done in the presence of an acid catalyst such as sulfuric acid, at 80-300C
* Almost always RA2+RB2, sometimes ARB

### [[Polyamides]]
* Happens when a dicarboxylic acid reacts with di-amine
* This is analogous to polyester formation and this we won't discuss the mechanism and equation further
* Aliphatic polyamides are called nylons and are mostly formed by RA2+RB2 polycondensation
