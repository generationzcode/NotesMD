
* Most high viscosity fluids are pseudoplastic
* High shear rate impellers are not used because the high shear rate is only noticed in the immediate vicinity of the impeller, because of the fluids pseudoplastic nature
* anchor and helical impellers are used. Anchor impellers are better with heat transfer and helical are better at power consumption, top to bottom mixing
* impeller shear rates are always higher than process shear rates and impeller viscosity for pseudoplastic fluids is always lower than the process viscosity
* to get the impeller viscosity (the viscosity the impeller sees due to the non newtonian nature of the fluid), one calculates the power number, which is experimentally found from the speed measurements and the reynolds number and relates the two through a relation to get viscosity. This is also the "apparent viscosity"
* to get the impeller shear rate, relate it with the viscosity
