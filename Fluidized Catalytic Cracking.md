## History
- McAfee made a batch catalytic reactor. Catalyst was AlCl3
- Houdry made a continuous catalytic cracker with multiple fixed bed reactors.
- AlCl3 was expensive and corrosive, houdry had cheap catalysts like alumina, clay, silica particles
- 1942 - thermofor catalytic cracking. First process that involved a moving bed of catalysts. Bucket elevated system
- UOP stacked unit - regenerator at the bottom, bucket elevated system. Reactor at the top. Catalyst is moved, not the product.
- When you move the catalyst from the reactor to the regenerator, you use air. You dont want hydrocarbon to enter since if it contacts the air, explosion can occur

## Delta coke
- its the difference in coke in the regenerator and the reactor. The below is what the delta coke is comprised of
- Catalytic coke is the coke deposit formed when the hydrocarbons are cracked via acid site catalysis
- Cat/oil - adsorbed or unstripped hydrocarbons entrained by catalyst flowing through the stripper
- Contaminant - coke produced as byproduct of contaminant metal dehydrogenation activity
- Additive - increased coke due to feed conradson carbon that goes directly to the coke on the catalyst
## Effect of increasing reactor temperature
- (increases catalyst circulation)
- increases cat/oil ratio
- increases regenerator temperature - since less coke is being formed
- increases conversion
- increases or decreases gasoline depending on overcracking point
- Increases the gasoline octane and bromine number (measure of olefins)
## Factors that reduce delta coke
- An increase in cat/oil ratio

## Flue gas
- It's partially oxidised and efforts should be made to increase the oxidised amount of CO
- The temperature of flue gas is higher than inside the regenerator because of an effect of afterburning
- The flue gas burns for a bit more than it's residence time
- If you're using a lighter material in the cracker, less coke is formed
- Since less coke is formed, less is burnt. This causes the bed temperature to decrease
- Here, we use an external source of heat in place of coke
- The external source of heat is called a torch oil (injected)

## Impact of feedstock on performance of FCCU
- Coke forming tendancy affects the heat balance
- aromatic feeds have a higher coke forming capacity
- conradson carbon content of the feed conversely affects the coke selectivity
- nickel and vanadium content of the charge also influence the coke yield by causing dehydrogenation of heavy products that can form contaminant coke
- Chemical composition of the feedstock affects yields and quality of products
- aromatic feeds produce less gasoline than paraffinic feeds but gasoline has a higher octane number because it is more aromatic
- Sulfur - sulfur in coke is converted into sox in the regenerator. SOX treatement of flue gas needed
