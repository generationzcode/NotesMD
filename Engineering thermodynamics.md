## Another Perspective

> The above notes are from my professor's perspective, what he taught in class. The following is what I understand of thermodynamics. I may be wrong

* An addition to what was said in the introduction of the previous part - The reason we study thermodynamics as chemical engineers. The heat machines are briefly discussed to give awareness, if we were to go deeper into it, we'd be looking at valves, rotation, clearances - the more technical aspects. Here we summarize all those components as S_{gen}, not to understand how they work, but to understand their effects. The two types of problems, then that we'd be equipped to solve would be - understanding the best case scenario for a certain situation and analysing a real situation and quantifying the inefficiencies. 
* The real stuff we're supposed to learn are mixtures. The number of mixtures out there is in the trillions, very few have had their properties scientifically measured, so a lot of the work we chemical engineers have to do to design a new reactor, or tank is with mixtures that havent been previously studied. Thermodynamics here simply helps us extrapolate from data accurately to get to the mixture properties of any substances at any temperature and pressure.
