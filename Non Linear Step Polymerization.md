#polymers 
This forms network molecules.
A network molecule is a molecule with degree of polymerization is infinity, the point when such a molecule is formed is called the gel point and the formation of this gel point is called gelation. At the gel point, the mixture suddenly, from viscous stops being viscous and shows resistance and incapability to flow

## Formaldehyde based resins
![[Pasted image 20240910184103.png]]
formaldehyde urea and formaldehyde melamine resins are also popular and follow a similar process. Phenol has a functionality of 3, urea of 4 and melamine of 6. The formaldehyde substitutes on the phenol on ortho or para positions due to the hydroxyl group activating the phenol ring

two kinds of formaldehyde phenol resins exist - resoles and novolaks
* Resole - made using an excess of formaldehyde and base catalyzed reaction causing many unreacted methylol groups to remain on the phenol, the prepolymer is then put in a mold and the methylol groups react to form network polymers
* Novolaks - made using an excess of phenol and acid catalyzed reaction causing no unreacted methylol groups to remain and the prepolymer is unable to cross link. The prepolymer is then ground into powder and compunded with some fillers, then a catalyst is added and it is put in a heated mold to crosslink
## Epoxy Resins
these are formed using an epoxy end group prepolymer and a molecule with amine groups (more than 2). The tri or more amine molecule is highly functional and cures the epoxy prepolymer. The chain length of the epoxy prepolymer and the R group in the amine molecule determine the mechanical properties of the epoxy resin
