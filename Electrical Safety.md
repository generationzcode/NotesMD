* Static electricity causes events
> [!example]
> A fire and a series of explosions occurred in a chemical distribution facility. The fire started in a packaging area while a 300 gallon tank was being filled with ethyl acetate. An operator place the fill nozzle in the opening on the top of the tank and suspended a steel veil on it to keep it in place. As the tank was filling the operator heard a popping sound and saw it engulfed in flames. The nozzle was laying on the floor spilling ethyl acetate. Employees tried unsuccessfully to extinguish the fire, couldnt do so. The are was evacuated. The fire spread to a warehouse igniting other stored liquids. Because of the smoke and the rocketing barrels, nearby businesses were evacuated. 
> It was determined that an ignitable vapour air mixture formed near the tote fill opening. While the body of the tank, the weigh scale and the pump were grounded. The steel weight, hose pump and the steel parts of the nozzle was not grounded. Static electricity probably accumulated on these parts and caused a spark and ignited the whole thing

* Static electricity is formed when liquid flows through pipes, valves and other equipment
* Correct bonding and grounding ensures that static electricity does not accumulate and cause a spark
* Static sparks can ignite many flammable vapour air mixtures
* **Bonding** refers to connecting materials to make sure they're same electrical potential
* **Grounding** refers to connecting the material to a 0 potential. The earth, we consider as 0 potential
## How to prevent an event
* Ensure conductive piping is properly bonded and grounded. and properly designed for flammable service
* When carrying out tasks, ensure as little free fall as possible to prevent sparks
