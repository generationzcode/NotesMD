#polymers 
Simple - uses monomer and monomer soluble initiator. The monomer is where the reactions occur for polymerization, not in any other medium
This is good for high purity, high molar mass products, good for polycondensation. Polyaddition is better suited for [[Solution Polymerization]], its also good for polycondensation

Issues with bulk polymerization are with heat transfer (if heat is generated, it's difficult to remove and causes autoacceleration) and viscosity buildup. These are solved by only allowing low conversion.

Polystyrene is an example of a polymer made using bulk polymerization
Advantages
* High yield per reactor volume
* Easy Polymer Recovery
* The option of casting the polymerization mixture into final product form
Limitations:
* Difficulty in removing last traces of monomer
* The problem of dissipating heat produced during the reaction. This can be improved by providing special baffles
* The problem of viscosity buildup in the reactor
