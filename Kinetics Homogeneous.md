A homogeneous reaction is a reaction where all reactants are of the same phase
* rate expression is expressed as $-r_{A} = kC^{a}_{A}C^{b}_B...$ 
* the powers of each concentration is usually same as molecularity of that substance in the reaction for *elementary reactions*
* temperature dependency of k is $$k = Ae^{-\frac{E}{RT}}$$ Where E is the activation energy of the reaction, R is the ideal gas constant and T is the temperature in kelvin. A is a factor that represents number of collisions and is also called the frequency factor
* As it gets more complicated, just use common sense man.
* Series reactions can be modeled by simply taking the each reaction and calculating the rate for every intermediate
* Same with parallel

## Flow reactors
* Unlike batch reactors, the real biggies are the continuous flow reactors that keep taking in chemicals and outputting chemicals continuously
* There are two types of flow reactors - plug flow and continuous stir tank reactors(AKA backmix reactors)
* The basic equations have symbols F = molar flow rate, C = concentration, V = control volume
