 * The prisoners dilemma - see it somewhere
* The assumption in most descriptions of this is that all players are greedy and selfish. The game frame here is incomplete, since the preferences of individual players are not given
* A player could be benevolent and fair and have a different picking of game strategies that might lead to a different overall result
* The right way to go about solving this is to obtain a utility function (a list of numbers that give the satisfaction a player gets from a certain strategy), based on the utility function, draw the matrix and solve for whichever strategy is strictly dominant
