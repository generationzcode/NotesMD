## A Brief History
* 1820 -> This guy, Thomas Hancock, found a way to blend additives to rubber (a natural polymer) by beating it up (masticating it)         ![[Pasted image 20240713172639.png]]
* 1839 -> Charles Goodyear added sulphur to rubber and found it got stronger. This process was named vulcanization.
* 1846 - 1870 -> Some guy prepared nitrocellulose, sold it as an explosive. Another guy thought to mold it and make items out of it as it was found it be a hard elastic material that is also soluble and easily mold-able. Two other guys thought it would be smart to do this with something that isn't an explosive - celluloid. Instant commercial success.
* 1892 -> Cross, Bevan and Beadle patented the viscose process for dissolving and regenerating cellulose
* 1920s -> This absolute chad of a human, Hermann Staudinger studied the synthesis, structure and properties of polyoxymethylene and polystyrene to give emprical evidence of his idea that polymers are really just macromolecules  containing large sequences of monomers. Everyone doubted him, but the haters were proven wrong
