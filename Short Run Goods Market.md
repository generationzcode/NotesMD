- GDP is basically the total value added in an economy in a year.
- Economists divide up the GDP into a few categories of value
- Consumption - goods and services purchased by consumers. 
- Investment - purchase of new plants, new machines (non residential) and apartments and houses (residential)
- Government spending - purchases of goods and services by the government
- There is also the difference between the production and sales, which is the inventory investment. 
- Also keep in mind imports and exports, as in goods and services bought from other economies and sold to other economies
- Now we can write demand as:
- $$Z = C+I+G+X-IM$$
- where X stands for exports
- Assuming the economy is closed so X=IM=0
- $$Z=C+I+G$$
## Consumption
- It's a function of disposable income
- Disposable income is income-taxes, so we can express C as:
- $$C = c_0+c_1(Y-T)$$
- c_1 is the marginal propensity to consume, with every additional rupee of disposable income, consumption increases by c_1
## Investment
- This is an exogenous variable
- There are various types of variables in a model, endogenous variables depend on other variables in the model, exogenous variables don't 
- $$I=\bar{I}$$
## Government Spending
- Taxes and government spending describe **fiscal policy**
- T and G are both exogenous variables.

## Equilibrium Output
- $$Z=C+I+G$$
- $$Z = c_0+c_1(Y-T)+I+G$$
- Equilibrium in the goods market means production equals demand and that firms do not hold inventories. Thus inventory investment is always equal to zero. 
- Production(Y), then must be equal to the demand for goods (Z), Y=Z
- So:
- $$Y=c_0+c_1(Y-T)+I+G$$
- Getting it in terms of equilibrium production (Y):
- $$Y=\frac{1}{1-c_1}(c_0+I+G-c_1T)$$
- Now the terms in the brackets are all exogenous, and are called autonomous spending. They are not dependent on demand
- The fraction they are multiplied by is called the multiplier
## The multiplier
- The cleanest way to understand the multiplier is a simple one. Imagine the c_0 term of the consumption equation goes up by some amount. Consumption then, must increase. An increase in consumption causes an increase in production. An increase in production causes an increase in Y, thus an increase in disposable income, thus it causes another increase in consumption. This keeps going on and on until it reaches an equilibrium value. Multiplying the initial increase in consumption with the multiplier will give you the final equilibrium value
- $$1+c_1+c_1^2+...+c_1^n$$
- When c_1 is less than 1, the above evals to $$\frac{1}{1-c_1}$$, which is the multiplier

## IS relation
- Disposable income - consumption = savings
- $$S = Y-T-C$$
- Public savings is: $T-G$
- $$Y=C+\bar I+G$$
- $$Y-T-C=\bar I +G -T$$
- $$S=I+G-T$$
- $$\bar I = (T-G)+S$$
- The above shows that public savings+consumers savings is equal to investment. At equilibrium, what the people and government want to save, firms want to invest
