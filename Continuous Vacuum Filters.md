* These use vacuum to create the pressure differential for the driving force for the filtration. Since vacuum can be maintained independent of the slurry being filtered, it's well suited for a continuous process as mentioned in the preceding page

>[!done] Checklist
>* Liquor is sucked through a moving septum to deposit a cake of solids
>* the cake is removed out of the filtering zone, washed, dried, removed from the septum
>* pressure differential around 250-500 mmHg
>* Vacuum applied from a stationary source to the moving parts

## Rotary Drum Filter
![[Pasted image 20250115165514.png]]
(image from science direct, thanks guys)
* Most common type
* Drum turns in an agitated slurry trough
* Inside the drum is another drum and the sucking of the slurry happens in the annular volume between the two drums
* Drums connected to the vacuum source through a rotary valve
